💡 Business Insights — Sales Analytics Dashboard (2014–2017)
Insights derived from the Sales Analytics Dashboard built in Power BI,
analyzing Superstore retail data from January 2014 to December 2017.
1. 📱 Phones & Chairs Dominate Sub-Category Sales
Phones and Chairs are the top-performing sub-categories, each generating $0.33M in sales
Together they account for a significant share of total revenue
Recommendation: Prioritize inventory and marketing spend on these two sub-categories for maximum ROI
2. 👥 Consumer Segment Leads in Volume
The Consumer segment has the highest number of items sold (~4K+), significantly ahead of Corporate and Home Office
Despite high volume, profit margin should be monitored — high sales don't always mean high profit
Recommendation: Analyze if discount strategies in the Consumer segment are hurting profit margins
3. 🗺️ Regional Performance Gap
West and East regions outperform Central and South in total sales across all categories
The area chart shows a clear downward trend from West → South
Recommendation: Investigate underperformance in Central and South — could be logistics, pricing, or market penetration issues
4. 💻 Technology is the Highest Revenue Category
Technology accounts for 32.3% ($742K) of total sales
Furniture follows at 36.4% ($836K) — highest share but check if profit margin is proportional
Office Supplies at 31.3% ($719K) — steady performer
Recommendation: Technology has high revenue potential — expanding the product line could boost overall sales

6. 💰 Profit Margin is Low at 12%
With $2.30M in sales but only $286.40K in profit, the profit margin is just 12%
This suggests either high discounting, high costs, or both
Recommendation: Conduct a deeper discount analysis — identify which categories or regions are being over-discounted
7. 📦 Home Office Segment is Underserved
Home Office has the lowest sales and item count across all segments
Yet post-2020 trends show massive growth in home office demand (outside this dataset's range)
Recommendation: Home Office segment represents an untapped growth opportunity
